The honest version

How a guide actually comes together.

Not every guide on this site started with me getting on a plane. Here's the real difference between the two ways one gets made.

If I've visited a place myself, the guide is built on that trip. If I haven't, it's built on people I trust who have, plus a lot of careful digging through what's already out there. I think you're owed knowing which is which, so here's exactly how both work.

Two ways a guide gets made

When I've been there

One real trip, used properly. Nothing here is stretched into something it isn't.

01

The trip itself is the main source

I walk the city, eat at the places I'd genuinely recommend, and take notes the whole way through.

02

Practical details get checked on the ground

Opening hours, prices, and how to actually get around come from being there, not from guessing.

03

One visit, written up honestly

I don't pad it out with claims of repeat visits or weeks spent there. It's one proper trip, used well.

When I haven't (yet)

Built from people who've actually been, and a lot of digging to back it up.

01

My network first

I ask people I know who've been there for their itinerary, what surprised them, and what they'd skip next time.

02

Then deep research

I go through blogs, forums, discussion threads, and review sites, looking for what keeps coming up rather than one lone opinion.

03

Cross-checked, not copied

If several independent sources agree, it goes in the guide. If they don't, I dig further or leave it out entirely.

True for every guide

Every guide gets the same scrutiny

Whether it comes from my own trip or from contacts and research, it goes through the same checks before it's published.

Written by one person

No outsourced writing and no contributors. I put together every guide myself, start to finish.

Updated when things change

Guides get rechecked over time, especially whenever a reader flags something through the bug report form.

What I can't promise

A guide built from a network and careful research is only as good as the contacts and sources behind it. It isn't the same as me having stood there myself, and I'd rather say that plainly than pretend otherwise.

If something's off in either kind of guide, the fastest fix is telling me directly through the bug report form.
