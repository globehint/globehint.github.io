Overview

Cologne is a city of surprising contrasts, where the ancient world meets a modern, vibrant pulse. Dominated by its magnificent Gothic cathedral, the city boasts a rich Roman heritage, a thriving arts scene, and a unique beer culture centered around its local Kölsch brew. Expect a welcoming, lively atmosphere with plenty to explore, from historic lanes to trendy districts.

Best time to visit: May-September for pleasant weather and outdoor events, or December for the festive Christmas markets. Most people spend 3-4 days, though you could comfortably stretch to 5-7 days with day trips to Bonn and Düsseldorf. Comfortable walking shoes are a must, especially for the Altstadt and the cathedral climb. Layers are also advisable year-round.

Before you book

Book accommodation well in advance, especially if visiting during major events like Carnival (February/March), the Christmas markets (December), or large trade fairs (e.g. Gamescom, imm cologne).

Closed on certain days

Most museums in Cologne close on Mondays. Many smaller shops and some independent restaurants also close on Sundays, so plan your itinerary accordingly.

Accessibility

The city centre and areas along the Rhine are largely flat. However, the historic Altstadt features numerous cobblestone streets which can be challenging. Many U-Bahn (subway) stations have lifts or ramps, but not all; trams are generally more accessible.

Common scams to know

Be aware of pickpocketing in crowded tourist areas like around the Cathedral and the main train station, especially during festivals or busy market times. Always keep an eye on your belongings.

Attractions - the full list

The unmissable four

Cologne Cathedral (Kölner Dom)

Altstadt-Nord

A UNESCO World Heritage site and Germany's most visited landmark, this colossal Gothic masterpiece took over 600 years to complete. Its twin spires dominate the city skyline, and the interior is equally breathtaking with stunning stained glass windows and intricate carvings.

Cathedral Free, Tower ~€6, Treasury ~€61-3 hoursCathedral 6am-7:30pm (Nov-Apr), 6am-9pm (May-Oct)Tower 9am-5pm (Nov-Feb), 9am-6pm (Mar-Apr, Oct), 9am-6:30pm (May-Sep)
Tip: Climb the South Tower (533 steps!) first thing in the morning to avoid the longest queues and enjoy a quieter view of the city.

Ludwig Museum (Museum Ludwig)

Altstadt-Nord

Located right next to the Cathedral, this impressive modern art museum houses one of Europe's most important collections of Pop Art, including a significant number of works by Picasso, as well as German Expressionism and Russian Avant-Garde art. It's a must for art lovers.

~€142-3 hoursTue-Sun 10am-6pmClosed Mon
Tip: Don't miss the views of the Cathedral from the museum's upper floors and outdoor terraces. Check their website for special exhibitions, which often require separate tickets.

Old Town (Altstadt)

Altstadt-Nord

Wander through the charming, narrow cobblestone streets of Cologne's historic Old Town, stretching along the Rhine. You'll find colourful, traditional houses, numerous Kölsch breweries, and lively squares like Alter Markt and Heumarkt. It's the heart of Cologne's character.

Free2-4 hoursAlways openNone
Tip: Find a spot at an outdoor table at a traditional brewery like Früh am Dom or Peters Brauhaus. Order a Kölsch and soak in the atmosphere, watching the unique Köbes (waiters) at work.

Hohenzollern Bridge (Hohenzollernbrücke)

Deutz

This iconic railway bridge, adorned with hundreds of thousands of "love locks," offers the most postcard-perfect views of the Cologne Cathedral and the city skyline. It's a key pedestrian link across the Rhine and a symbol of the city's romantic spirit.

Free30-60 minsSunset
Tip: Walk across the bridge from the Deutz side (east bank) towards the Hauptbahnhof (main station) for the most breathtaking, uninterrupted views of the Cathedral, especially at sunset.

Also genuinely worth your time

Chocolate Museum (Schokoladenmuseum)

Rheinauhafen

A fun and educational museum dedicated to the history and production of chocolate, from ancient Mayan cocoa rituals to modern manufacturing. Includes a small chocolate factory and a giant chocolate fountain for tasting.

~€14.501.5-2 hoursTue-Sun 10am-6pm (Mon 10am-6pm from Apr-Oct)

Wallraf-Richartz Museum

Altstadt-Süd

Home to an impressive collection of European art spanning from the Middle Ages to the early 20th century, including works by Rubens, Rembrandt, and Impressionist masters. It's one of Germany's oldest museums.

~€122-3 hoursTue-Sun 10am-6pm

EL-DE Haus (NS Documentation Centre)

City Centre

Housed in the former Gestapo headquarters, this museum offers a sobering and important look at Cologne's history during the Nazi era. The preserved prison cells with inmate graffiti are particularly poignant.

~€4.501.5-2 hoursTue-Fri 10am-4pm, Sat-Sun 11am-4pm

Botanical Garden Flora und Botanischer Garten Köln

Riehl

A stunning oasis featuring diverse plant collections, historic glasshouses, and beautifully landscaped gardens. Perfect for a peaceful stroll away from the city bustle.

Gardens Free, Glasshouse ~€61-2 hoursDaily, 8am-8pm (summer), 8am-4:30pm (winter)

Budget note

Accommodation prices in Cologne can skyrocket during major trade fairs (like Gamescom or imm cologne) and during Carnival season. Book several months in advance or consider staying in nearby towns if your visit coincides with these events.

Walking

Cologne is largely flat around the river and city centre, making it a very pleasant city to explore on foot. The Altstadt and areas around the Cathedral are particularly compact and best discovered by walking. Be prepared for cobblestone streets in historic areas, so comfortable footwear is a must.

Eat

Where to eat & drink

Cologne's food scene is deeply rooted in hearty Rhineland cuisine, best enjoyed with a glass of local Kölsch beer. Expect traditional German fare, but also a diverse international influence.

Kölsch & Himmel un Ääd

You can't talk about Cologne's food and drink without mentioning **Kölsch**, the city's unique pale ale. It's served in small, thin "Stange" glasses by traditional waiters called Köbes. For a classic dish, try **Himmel un Ääd** (Heaven and Earth), a comforting plate of mashed potatoes (earth) and apple sauce (heaven) typically served with fried black pudding and crispy onions.

Coffee/drink culture

Cologne's drink culture is dominated by Kölsch beer. In traditional breweries, Köbes (waiters) will automatically replace your empty Stange glass with a full one until you place your coaster on top of your glass to signal you're finished. For coffee, expect good quality espresso-based drinks in most cafes, often served with a small piece of chocolate.

Good to know

The German "ch" sound can be tricky for English speakers. It's often a soft, guttural sound (as in "Bach") or a softer "sh" sound (as in "ich"). Don't worry too much though, most Germans will understand your efforts!

Know before you go
  • Master the Kölsch ritual: In traditional breweries, Köbes (waiters) will automatically replace your empty Kölsch glass. To signal you're finished, place your coaster on top of your glass.
  • Cash is still king (sometimes): While card payments are common, many smaller shops, cafes, and especially traditional breweries still prefer or only accept cash. Always carry some Euros.
  • Carnival is a big deal: If visiting in February or March, be aware that Carnival is a major festival. It's incredibly lively but also means higher prices and crowded streets.
  • Sunday closures: Most shops are closed on Sundays, except for those at the main train station. Plan your shopping accordingly to avoid disappointment.
  • Book brewery tours: If you're keen on learning more about Kölsch brewing, book a tour at one of the traditional breweries (like Früh or Reissdorf) in advance, especially on weekends.
  • Embrace the "Veedel" culture: Cologne is proud of its distinct neighbourhoods (Veedel). Explore beyond the Altstadt to experience different local vibes, from trendy Ehrenfeld to the bohemian Belgian Quarter.
