Start here

Overview

Perched high in the Bavarian Alps, Berchtesgaden pairs postcards of pristine mountain beauty with some of the heaviest history of the twentieth century. The primary draw is the Eagle's Nest, a towering stone lodge built on a precipitous ridge for the Nazi party's elite. Below the peaks lies a beautifully preserved alpine town filled with pastel facades and the faint scent of roasting pork and fresh pine. It is a place where jaw-dropping engineering marvels stand alongside sobering historical records.

Best time to visit: Mid-May to October. A full day is enough to see the highlights; aim to leave Munich by 7:30am to make the most of it. Bring sturdy walking shoes and layer up, as temperatures at the mountain summit can drop sharply compared to the valley below.

Day trip or overnight?

A full day trip covers the essentials comfortably, allowing you to scale the Eagle's Nest and wander the town center. However, if you want to take an electric boat cruise across the emerald waters of nearby Lake Königssee or tour the old salt mines, staying one night opens up those incredible valley sites without rushing.

Before you go

The Eagle's Nest is completely closed during the winter and early spring due to heavy alpine snow. Always check the official website for opening dates before booking transit, and purchase your specialized mountain bus tickets in advance online to secure a precise time slot.

Closed on certain days

While the mountains and outdoor scenery never close, many independent shops and traditional eateries in Berchtesgaden town shut down early on Saturday afternoons and remain completely closed on Sundays. Plan your trip for a weekday or Saturday morning to experience the town at its liveliest.

Accessibility

The Eagle's Nest has reasonable accessibility; specialized low-floor buses handle the ascent, and the historic brass elevator is wheelchair-friendly. However, the mountain summit trail is gravelly and steep, and Berchtesgaden's old town features rolling hills with traditional cobblestone surfaces.

Common scams to know

Scams are rare in this rural alpine region, but look out for unauthorized private tour operators at transit hubs charging inflated rates for "guaranteed" Eagle's Nest access when official tickets are sold out. Stick entirely to the official RVO bus network.

The unmissable few

The Eagle's Nest (Kehlsteinhaus)

Kehlstein Summit

Hitler's infamous mountaintop retreat sits precariously at 1,834 meters. Built as a lavish 50th birthday gift by the Nazi party, the stone building survived Allied bombing raids intact. Visitors walk through a chilly 124-meter stone tunnel to ride a gleaming, original brass elevator up through the center of the rock mountain directly into the historic lodge.

Around €32 (includes bus and lift)2-3 hours8:30am - 4:00pmClosed November to May
Tip: Book the earliest morning bus slot possible. If clouds roll in later in the afternoon, your sweeping view of the Salzburg valley can vanish entirely into a dense wall of white mist.

Obersalzberg Documentation Center

Obersalzberg Valley

Located at the base mountain station where the specialized summit buses depart, this highly detailed museum provides essential historical context. It thoroughly chronicles how this quiet mountain community was systematically seized and converted into a massive second seat of power for the Third Reich. The most haunting feature is exploring the vast, dark underground concrete bunker complexes.

€61.5-2 hours9:00am - 5:00pmOpen daily
Tip: Visit this center immediately after descending from the Eagle's Nest; it anchors the stunning mountaintop vistas in their proper, sobering historical reality.

Berchtesgaden Old Town (Altstadt)

Town Center

A beautifully framed alpine town that feels a world away from the heavy monuments up the mountain. The heart of the village is packed with traditional Bavarian houses adorned with Lüftlmalerei (elaborate, colorful wall murals). Wander through quiet pedestrian squares, watch locals in traditional dress, and enjoy the dramatic mountain backdrops framing every street corner.

Free1-2 hours24/7Shops closed Sundays
Tip: Head into the inner courtyard of the Royal Castle (Schlossplatz) to view the oldest arcade arches in the town and escape the busy main commercial streets.

Also genuinely worth your time

Berchtesgaden Salt Mine (Salzbergwerk)

Town Outskirts

Operating continuously since 1517, this underground salt mine offers an incredibly fun, deep cave tour. Visitors don miner's overalls, ride a miniature train into the dark earth, slide down long wooden miner's slides, and cross a silent underground salt lake via a wooden raft.

Around €241.5 hours9:00am - 5:00pm

Stiftskirche St. Peter und Paul

Schlossplatz

This striking double-towered church dates back to the twelfth century and anchors the historic town center. The interior blends austere Romanesque foundations with ornate Gothic and Baroque artistic additions, offering a deeply peaceful place to pause.

Free30 minsDaily until dusk

The King's Cottage (Königliches Schloss)

Schlossplatz

A former Augustinian monastery that later served as the summer residence for the Bavarian Wittelsbach royal family. The interior rooms display fine historical furniture, delicate porcelain, and a notable collection of classic alpine hunting weapons.

Around €121 hourVaries seasonally

Tickets & passes

The excellent Deutschland-Ticket or a regional Bayern-Ticket (Bavaria Ticket) fully covers the regional train journey to Berchtesgaden station. However, note that these tickets do not cover the specialized RVO mountain bus (Line 849) required to climb up to the Eagle's Nest itself. The last direct train connection back to Munich typically leaves Berchtesgaden around 8:00pm.

Do you need a car?

No. The train line delivers you straight to the Berchtesgaden station, where local buses connect seamlessly to the base of the mountain and the old town is entirely walkable.

Guided or independent?

Independent travel is very straightforward if you use the trains, but an organized tour can save you the hassle of coordinating multiple bus connections between the sights.

Getting around once you arrive

The historical old town of Berchtesgaden is incredibly compact and best explored on foot, but navigating between the town, the valley station, and the summit requires using local buses.

Eat

Where to eat & drink

Dining in Berchtesgaden focuses heavily on classic, comforting upper-Bavarian tavern fare designed to satisfy hungry mountain hikers.

Midday kitchen closure

Unlike Munich, many traditional kitchens in this mountain town close strictly between 2:00pm and 5:30pm, meaning you need to time your lunch stop carefully or rely on mountain-top snack kiosks.

The Great Schnitzel Debate

Locals argue fiercely over who serves the crispiest pork schnitzel in town. Traditionalists demand it fried purely in clarified butter, while contemporary spots try adding modern twists like horseradish marinades.

Coffee/drink culture

In the late afternoon, locals gather for Kaffee und Kuchen (coffee and cake). Order a large filter coffee and a slice of regional plum cake, always topped with a generous dollop of fresh whipped cream.

Staying for dinner?

It is well worth having an early dinner in the old town before catching your train back to Munich. The atmosphere in the taverns grows incredibly cozy as the day-tripping crowds filter out, leaving the warm dining rooms to the locals.

The most photogenic spots

Between the massive alpine drops and the colorful historic streets, keeping your camera ready is a requirement.

  • The Kehlstein Summit CrossWalk just above the Eagle's Nest building to capture the dramatic contrast of the iron cross against the endless mountain peaks.
  • Schlossplatz SquareStand in the center of the square to frame the ancient painted house facades against the massive wall of Mount Watzmann.
  • The Brass Elevator TunnelUse the dramatic, symmetrical natural stone archways of the entry tunnel for a moody, historical perspective.

Know before you go

Tips & tricks

  • Keep a very close eye on the train timetable; if you miss the final evening connection back toward Freilassing, you will face an incredibly expensive taxi fare across regional borders.
  • Coin-operated luggage lockers are available inside the main Berchtesgaden train station if you need to store heavy backpacks before boarding the mountain buses.
  • Cash remains king in traditional alpine taverns and mountain kiosks; always carry small Euro banknotes as many places refuse foreign credit cards for smaller bills.
  • The Eagle's Nest can close unexpectedly during summer if a sudden alpine storm or heavy high-altitude snowfall compromises the mountain roads.
  • Pack a windproof jacket and sunglasses; the bright alpine glare at the summit is intense and weather conditions can change in a matter of minutes.
  • Be respectful when photographing the historical sites; local guides are highly sensitive to the historical gravity of the Obersalzberg area.
