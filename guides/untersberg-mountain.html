Start here

Overview

Rising sharply above the southern horizon of Salzburg, Untersberg Mountain is a majestic limestone massif steeped in myths of sleeping emperors and hidden caverns. A modern cable car effortlessly whisks you from the valley base up to an elevation of 1,776 meters in just under ten minutes. From the summit ridge, you are treated to a jaw-dropping contrast of urban architecture unfolding into the jagged, snow-dusted Berchtesgaden Alps. The crisp alpine air, the whistling mountain wind, and the expansive 360-degree views make it the easiest wilderness escape from the city center.

Best time to visit: May to October for ideal hiking conditions and peak operations. A half day is plenty of time to see the highlights, though you should aim to leave Salzburg by 8:30am to beat the afternoon clouds and tour groups. Sturdy shoes with excellent grip are absolutely essential, and bring a warm outer layer because temperatures at the top are significantly lower than down in the city valley.

Day trip or overnight?

A half-day trip covers the essentials comfortably, leaving you plenty of time to explore the city in the afternoon. However, if serious high-alpine endurance trekking or making the multi-hour hike over to Germany's side appeals, starting early is key, though staying overnight is rarely necessary given how close it is to Salzburg.

Before you go

Check the live webcam on the official Untersbergbahn website before boarding the bus from town. It is common for the valley to look clear while the mountain summit is completely blanketed in thick fog, hiding the view.

Closed on certain days

The cable car does not close on specific weekdays, but it goes completely offline for seasonal maintenance during early spring (typically March) and late autumn (November into December). Always verify the operational windows on the website prior to making the trek down.

Accessibility

The valley base station and top terminal are partially barrier-free with ramps and stairlifts. However, the outdoor alpine terrain beyond the mountain terrace consists of rough gravel, steep rocky steps, and sheer drops that are completely inaccessible for wheelchairs.

Common scams to know

There are no active tourist scams on the mountain, but look out for private tour companies trying to sell overpriced transit packages in Salzburg. Independent public buses are highly efficient and affordable.

The unmissable few

Untersberg Cable Car (Untersbergbahn)

St. Leonhard

The breathtaking cable car ride takes you from the valley base directly up the vertical rocky face of the mountain. The rapid change in perspective offers an incredible look at the geological scale of the region.

€34.00 Round Trip10 mins8:30am - 5:30pm (Summer)Closed for seasonal maintenance
Tip: The ride is 100% free with a Salzburg Card, allowing you to bypass the main ticket window and scan straight through the turnstiles.

Geiereck Peak & Summit Cross

Summit Area

Situated at 1,776 meters just a short distance from the top mountain station, this easily reachable peak features a giant wooden summit cross. It provides panoramic outlooks of the Salzburg basin and the winding Salzach river below.

Free10 min walkDaylight hoursNone
Tip: This is the most popular vantage point, so head here immediately after exiting the cable car to capture photos before other passengers gather.

Salzburg Hochthron Hike

Summit Trail

The trail leading to the highest accessible peak on the Austrian side of the ridge sits at 1,853 meters. The gravel path winds past alpine flora and reveals endless views of the rocky Bavarian peaks stretching far into Germany.

Free45 minsDaylight hoursImpassable in winter fog
Tip: Though well-maintained, the trail climbs up uneven limestone rocks; proper hiking boots are highly recommended over simple running shoes.

Also genuinely worth your time

Zeppezauerhaus Mountain Hut

Summit Ridge

An authentic alpine refuge situated just below the top station, serving traditional, hearty mountain food along with refreshing drinks on an open terrace looking out at the valley.

Varies20 min walkOpen May - October

Schellenberger Ice Cave

Bavarian Side

Germany's largest ice cave features grand underground frozen structures. It is an amazing place to see, but requires a very intense multi-hour hike from the summit station to reach.

€12.00 entry3-4 hours hikeGuided tours hourly in summer

Tickets & passes

Buying a 24-hour Salzburg Card for €38 to €42 is the ultimate transport hack. It covers your entire round-trip bus ride and the full price of the cable car ascent and descent, easily paying for itself on this single excursion. The final bus line 25 heading back to town generally leaves around 7:00pm.

Do you need a car?

No, taking public transit is actually faster and easier than navigating parking spaces at the base station. Bus 25 drops you directly at the cable car entrance stairs.

Guided or independent?

This trip is best done completely independently. A guided tour is unnecessary because the paths are well-marked and the bus journey is incredibly straightforward.

Coffee/drink culture

Order a Verlängerter (an espresso shot diluted with hot water) accompanied by a slice of homemade cake while sitting out on the sun-soaked decks of the mountain huts.

Staying for dinner?

The mountain huts wrap up food service as the last cable car departures approach. Head back down to Salzburg or Grödig for a traditional dinner in the evening.

In the mountains of Austria, it is a warm local custom to say "Servus" or "Berg frei" when passing other hikers along narrow trails.

Know before you go

Tips & tricks

  • The last cable car down leaves promptly at 5:30pm in summer; missing it means an incredibly steep, grueling, and hazardous 3-hour hike down on foot.
  • Store large bags in the luggage lockers at Salzburg Hauptbahnhof before boarding the bus; there are no locker facilities up on the mountain.
  • While the cable car ticket counter accepts credit cards, the mountain huts often prefer cash or have high minimum spending requirements for cards.
  • Always pack a lightweight windbreaker jacket; even on hot 30 degree Celsius days in Salzburg, the mountain peak frequently remains cold and breezy.
  • Do not wander off the marked paths; the massif contains deep limestone sinkholes and hidden drop-offs that are highly dangerous during sudden cloud cover.
