Overview

The Schwarzwaldhochstraße (B500) is Germany's oldest and most celebrated tourist route, a high-altitude ribbon of asphalt tracking the crest of the northern Black Forest. Starting directly above Baden-Baden, it serves up sweeping views of the Rhine Valley and France's Vosges Mountains on one side, and endless waves of dark, dense pine canopy on the other. It is a journey defined by misty glacial lakes, wind-swept moorland peaks, and classic alpine pit stops.

Best time to visit: May to October. Full day is enough to see the highlights - aim to leave Baden-Baden by 9:00 AM to make the most of it. Bring a sturdy outer shell and solid footwear; the ridge sits 1,000 metres higher than the valley floor and is frequently windy, foggy, and up to 8 degrees Celsius cooler than Baden-Baden.

Day trip or overnight?

A single day is perfect for covering the B500's primary lookouts and a short hike. Overnighting is only necessary if you want to tackle long-distance sections of the Westweg hiking trail or dive deep into the spa culture of Freudenstadt at the southern end of the highway.

Before you go

Download offline maps beforehand. Mobile reception along the heavily forested ridge can drop out entirely, particularly around the deep ravines and trailhead parking lots.

Closed on certain days

The scenic drive itself is always open, but the Mummelsee pedal boat rentals and some peak-top mountain huts close on Mondays or Tuesdays during the shoulder season (November to April).

Accessibility

The main hubs like Mummelsee and the National Park Centre Ruhestein have wheelchair-friendly paved paths. However, the climb to the Hornisgrinde summit and the wilder forest trails are steep, gravelly, and unsuitable for those with limited mobility.

Common scams to know

There are no classic tourist scams here, but watch out for aggressive tailgating by local drivers on the winding curves of the B500, and ensure you pay for parking at the official Mummelsee lot to avoid hefty municipal fines.

The unmissable few

Mummelsee

Seebach / B500

A legendary, dark glacial tarn steeped in folklore about underwater kings and mermaids. While highly touristy, it is an essential stop. You can walk the flat 15-minute loop around the shore or rent a pedal boat to venture onto the dark water.

Free1 hour24/7 (Shops 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM)None
Tip: Arrive before 10:00 AM or after 5:00 PM. Midday brings tour buses that choke the parking lot and ruin the eerie, quiet vibe of the lake.

Hornisgrinde Peak

Hornisgrinde

The highest mountain in the northern Black Forest (1,164m). A paved but steep trail leads from Mummelsee up to the summit plateau, which features a unique upland moor, wind turbines, and a historic lookout tower offering views of France.

Free (Tower €2)2 hours24/7 (Tower 11:00 AM – 5:00 PM)None
Tip: Walk the wooden boardwalk trail across the high moor (Grindenschwarzwald). It feels like walking through a sub-arctic tundra and offers the best unobstructed views.

Ruhestein National Park Centre

Ruhestein

A striking, modern architectural marvel that looks like fallen forest trees. Inside, it hosts an interactive exhibition about the wild, unmanaged forest ecosystem of the Black Forest National Park, plus a spectacular elevated skywalk.

€8 (Skywalk free)1.5 hours10:00 AM – 6:00 PMMondays
Tip: Walk the elevated skywalk trail behind the centre for a bird's-eye view of the canopy without paying for the indoor exhibition.

Also genuinely worth your time

Geroldsau Waterfall

Baden-Baden Outskirts

A picturesque, small waterfall nestled in a rhododendron-filled ravine. It is the perfect first stop just as you leave Baden-Baden to start climbing the B500.

Lothar Path (Lotharpfad)

Schliffkopf

A fascinating boardwalk trail built through a storm-damaged forest zone left untouched since Hurricane Lothar swept through in 1999, showcasing nature's self-healing power.

Free45 mins24/7

Sand

An active stop featuring a year-round bobsled-style alpine coaster, a climbing forest, and downhill trikes. Excellent for shaking off car sickness.

Tickets & passes

The best value for public transport is the RegioX Ticket (KVV). If you stay overnight in Baden-Baden, check if your hotel provides the KONUS Card, which gives you completely free travel on all regional buses, including the 245 climb up the B500. The last bus (Line 245) back to Baden-Baden from Mummelsee typically leaves around 5:45 PM.

Do you need a car?

No, but it is highly recommended. The bus runs reliably but limits your ability to make spontaneous stops at smaller trailheads like the Lothar Path or Geroldsau Waterfall.

Guided or independent?

Independent is best. The route is a single, well-marked highway (the B500), making navigation foolproof for drivers and bus passengers alike.

Getting around once you arrive

The B500 is a linear highway stretching over 60 kilometres. Once at major hubs like Mummelsee or Ruhestein, you will explore entirely on foot via well-marked dirt trails.

Public bathroom access

Clean, paid public toilets (€0.50 or €1.00) are available at the Mummelsee hotel complex and the National Park Centre Ruhestein. There are no toilets along the hiking trails, so use these main hubs.

Eat

Where to eat & drink

Dining along the ridge is rustic, heavy, and delicious. Expect hearty Swabian and Baden classics designed to fuel hikers: think Maultaschen, venison stew, and thick slices of cake.

Midday kitchen closure

Most mountain huts and traditional gasthofs serve hot food continuously from 11:30 AM to 8:00 PM, but smaller trailside cafés may close their kitchens between 2:30 PM and 5:30 PM, serving only coffee and cake.

The Black Forest Cake Debate

The local debate centers on the balance of ingredients in the iconic Schwarzwälder Kirschtorte (Black Forest Cherry Gateau). True purists argue that the cake must have a heavy hand of Kirschwasser (local cherry brandy) so that it packs a sharp, boozy punch, rather than being overly sweet and creamy.

Coffee/drink culture

Around 3:00 PM, locals practice Kaffee und Kuchen (coffee and cake). Along the B500, ordering a slice of Schwarzwälder Kirschtorte with a filter coffee or a Milchkaffee is practically mandatory.

Staying for dinner?

Most day-trippers head back to Baden-Baden for dinner, as the mountain ridge gets pitch black, foggy, and quiet after sunset. However, if you have a car, staying for a sunset dinner at the Grinde-Hütte is spectacular, provided you are comfortable driving the winding B500 in the dark.

Frame it

The most photogenic spots

The B500 offers dramatic elevation changes, meaning you will transition from misty pine tunnels to vast, open valley vistas within minutes.

  • Hornisgrinde Moor BoardwalkShoot facing west in the late afternoon; the golden light illuminates the yellow grasses of the high moor with the Vosges Mountains in the distance.
  • Mummelsee ShorelineCapture the classic shot of the wooden pier stretching into the dark, reflective water, ideally early in the morning when mist still clings to the surface.
  • Ellbachseeblick PlatformA spectacular wide-angle shot looking down over the pine canopy and the tiny, circular Ellbachsee lake directly below the wooden cantilevered deck.
  • Lotharpfad Storm PathWalk halfway along the boardwalk to photograph the chaotic beauty of fallen, moss-covered tree roots contrasting against vibrant new pine growth.

Good to know

In this region (Baden-Württemberg), you will often hear locals say "Tschüss" (bye) or the regional "Ade" (ah-day) when leaving shops or cafés. "Grüß Gott" is a common, polite greeting used when passing hikers on the trails.

Know before you go

Tips & tricks

  • Watch the last bus departure. If you are relying on Bus 245, the final service back to Baden-Baden from Mummelsee leaves around 5:45 PM. If you miss it, a taxi back down the mountain will cost over €100.
  • Store luggage in Baden-Baden. There are no luggage lockers or storage options along the B500 or at Mummelsee. Leave heavy bags at the Baden-Baden train station lockers.
  • Carry cash. Many of the rustic mountain huts (like Darmstädter Hütte) and parking ticket machines do not accept credit cards. Keep at least €20 to €30 in cash on hand.
  • Check the fog forecast. The B500 is notorious for sudden, dense fog (known locally as Nebel). If Baden-Baden is overcast, the ridge may be completely blanketed in white, obscuring all valley views. Check the Mummelsee webcam online before driving up.
  • Wear sturdy shoes. Even for short walks like the Lothar Path or the climb to Hornisgrinde, cobblestones, wooden grates, and gravel trails require shoes with decent grip. Leave the sandals in Baden-Baden.
  • Drive with headlights on. German law and safety customs dictate keeping your headlights on along the heavily shaded, winding forest curves of the B500, even in bright daylight.
