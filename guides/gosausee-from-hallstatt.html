Start here

Overview

Nestled in the Salzkammergut resort region, the Gosausee lakes offer a majestic natural contrast to the heavily touristed streets of nearby Hallstatt. The primary lake, Vorderer Gosausee, is celebrated for its glassy, mirror-like surface reflecting the massive Dachstein Glacier. A crisp mountain breeze carries the scent of alpine pines, while the distant clinking of cowbells underscores the tranquil rural atmosphere. It is an exceptional sanctuary for hikers, swimmers, and photography enthusiasts seeking raw alpine beauty.

Best time to visit: May to October. Half day is enough to see the highlights, though a full day allows for deeper trekking into the upper lakes. Aim to leave Hallstatt by 8:30am to make the most of it and beat the midday warmth. Wear sturdy hiking shoes with solid grip, as the terrain turns from paved paths to rocky alpine trails if you venture beyond the first lake.

Day trip or overnight?

A half-day trip covers the main lakeside walk and scenery comfortably, but if a multi-hour hike up to the secondary mountain lakes or ascending the high peaks via the via ferrata routes appeals, allocating a full day or staying one night nearby opens up the valley completely.

Before you go

Check the live mountain webcam and weather reports for the Dachstein region before departing. High-altitude clouds can roll in quickly, entirely obscuring the glacier view even if Hallstatt itself is perfectly sunny.

Closed on certain days

The main lake walking trails are open daily year-round, but the Gosaukamm cable car closes during seasonal maintenance windows in late spring and late autumn. Mountain restaurants also reduce hours during early May and late October, so pack your own snacks during shoulder months.

Accessibility

The loop trail surrounding the first lake (Vorderer Gosausee) is flat, wide, and entirely stroller and wheelchair friendly. However, the path climbing up toward the second lake is steep, rugged, and completely inaccessible without assistance.

Common scams to know

There are no active tourist scams in this protected nature zone. Be aware only of unmetered, private taxi drivers hanging around the Hallstatt bus terminal offering inflated flat-rate rides to the lake.

The unmissable few

Vorderer Gosausee

Lower Valley

The iconic front lake directly accessible from the parking area. Known globally for its flawless, crystal-clear water reflections of the jagged Dachstein mountain peak and its massive glacier field. A flat 4-kilometer path wraps entirely around its perimeter.

Free1-2 HoursOpen 24/7None
Tip: Walk along the eastern edge of the bank in the early morning for the absolute clearest, glass-like mountain reflections before the midday winds pick up.

Hinterer Gosausee

Upper Valley

The pristine, secluded rear lake hidden deep at the valley floor termination. Reaching it requires a steady uphill trek past a small middle pond, rewarding hikers with dramatic limestone canyon walls towering vertically on three sides and a rustic, working alpine dairy hut.

Free3 HoursBest May-OctWinter closures
Tip: Bring cash to purchase fresh buttermilk, local mountain cheese, and freshly baked bread served straight from the wooden alpine hut at the back lake.

Gosaukamm Cable Car

Valley Entrance

The mountain lift (Gosankammbahn) that whisks visitors effortlessly from the lower lake station up to the high Zwieselalm pastures. It opens up panoramic vistas overlooking the entire Gosau valley along with miles of higher mountain trekking tracks.

~€26 round-trip1-2 Hours8:15am - 4:50pmNov-May (Seasonal)
Tip: Buy a combined ticket if you plan to use multiple regional cable cars in Salzkammergut, and ensure you do not miss the final downward lift at late afternoon.

Also genuinely worth your time

Laserer Alpine Via Ferrata

Lake Border

A classic protected climbing route (Klettersteig) built directly into the rock face rising right over the lake waters. It features a spectacular suspension bridge crossing.

Free (Gear rental fees apply)1.5 HoursDaylight hours only

Gosaulacke

Middle Valley

The small, ephemeral intermittent lake situated between the front and rear lakes. It fills up primarily during spring snowmelt or heavy rainstorms, creating a surreal landscape.

Free30 MinsAccessible via hiking trail

Zwieselalm Hikes

Mountain Peak

A lush network of ridge trails branching out from the top of the cable car station, ranging from easy strolls to challenging ascents toward the Donnerkogel.

Free1-3 HoursBest during summer operations

The short trek over from Hallstatt into the Gosau Valley is highly straightforward and can be accomplished smoothly using either public buses or regional roads.

Tickets & passes

Standard bus fares can be paid in cash directly to the driver or purchased digitally via the OBB travel application. The final bus returning towards Hallstatt typically departs from the lake parking lot around 6:00pm during peak summer, so track your watch carefully to avoid an expensive private taxi ride.

Do you need a car?

No, the regional bus drop-off terminal sits directly at the edge of the front lake, placing you within immediate walking distance of all mountain paths and the lower cable car terminal.

Guided or independent?

This trip is best undertaken completely independently. Navigating the lakeside trails is intuitive, and exploring solo allows you to set your own hiking pacing around the upper lakes.

Getting around once you arrive

The entire alpine valley basin is entirely auto-free and must be explored purely on foot once you step past the primary parking lot zone.

Eat

Where to eat & drink

Dining here revolves around traditional, heavy Austrian alpine fare served inside historic wooden mountain taverns and rustic valley chalets.

Midday kitchen closure

The main sit-down restaurant at the front lake serves hot meals continuously all afternoon, but smaller high-altitude huts on the mountain ridges close kitchens between 2:30pm and 5:00pm.

The Kaiserschmarrn Debate

This classic torn alpine pancake dish is served at almost every valley outpost, with locals fiercely debating whether Gasthof Gosausee's caramelised version beats the lighter variation served high up at the mountain alpine huts.

Coffee/drink culture

After a long mountain walk, it is customary to order a Verlängerter (a traditional Austrian espresso diluted with hot water) alongside a generous slice of homemade apple strudel at a terrace table.

Staying for dinner?

Most visitors tend to journey back into Hallstatt for their evening dining experience, as the lakeside valley turns extremely quiet and menus shrink dramatically once the final afternoon tourist bus departs.

Frame it

The most photogenic spots

The entire valley is visual theater, but capturing the contrast between the turquoise water lines and the white glacial snowpack requires hitting specific vantage points.

  • The Boat Jetty PlatformShoot wide angles from the wooden pier right at sunrise to capture the undisturbed mountain reflection.
  • The Eastern Bank Pine FrameWalk 15 minutes along the loop path to frame the distant glacier through the overhanging evergreen boughs.
  • Hinterer Gosausee Valley FloorShoot from the far end of the rear lake looking backwards to capture the steep sheer canyon walls framing the shallow basin.
  • Zwieselalm CrestTake the lift up and face due south for an epic bird's-eye perspective over both the lower lake and the jagged mountain ridges.

Good to know

When hiking past fellow trekkers along the mountain trails, it is a firm local custom to cheerfully say "Servus" or "Griaß di" rather than a formal greeting.

Know before you go

Tips & tricks

  • Always verify the departure schedule for the final Bus 542 leaving Gosausee to avoid missing your connection back toward Hallstatt.
  • Carry plenty of cash coins and small banknotes, as the rustic mountain dairy outposts do not accept card payments.
  • Bring a swimming suit during July and August if you can brave the chilly mountain waters; swimming in Vorderer Gosausee is entirely free.
  • Arrive prior to 9:00am during the mid-summer months if driving yourself, as the close valley parking lots fill to maximum capacity early.
  • Pack a windbreaker jacket even in summer, as the proximity to the high glacier field causes temperatures to drop rapidly when clouds sweep in.
  • Do not approach or feed the alpine cattle grazing alongside the hiking tracks; stay strictly on the designated trails.
